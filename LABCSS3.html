Nightmare and Serenity

Brief Analysis of Touhou

Angel Mae T. Grueso

What is Touhou?

Touhou is a series of danmaku vertical shoot-em-up games. Players dodge beautiful, intricate bullet patterns. Set in Gensokyo, the games have a large cast, deep lore, and extensive fan content.

Who created Touhou?

Touhou was made by Team Shanghai Alice, solely run by ZUN (Jun’ya Ota). He’s been publishing Touhou since 1995, including collaborations with Twilight Frontier.

What is Touhou about?

The story is set in Gensokyo, a land once haunted by youkai before being sealed from the outside world in 1884 A.D.

Youkai are the dominant beings in Gensokyo, portrayed as antagonistic but actually coexist with humans in a symbiotic relationship. They need humans to maintain their identity and vice versa.

As spiritual entities, youkai are more affected by faith than physical injury. Some, like magicians, are more human-like. They follow certain codes—like honoring duels—and can live for centuries. They are spiritually tied to the Moon, as described by both Yukari Yakumo and Kaguya Houraisan, who offer different origin stories.

Over time, youkai have become more human in personality and form. Still, they remain uncanny compared to actual humans.
